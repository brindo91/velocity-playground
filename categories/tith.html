Toad in the hole is a traditional British dish consisting of sausages in Yorkshire pudding batter, served with buttery mash, gravy and vegetables. An all-time British classic, toad in the hole makes a knockout meal.

6

Ingredients

  • 8 Sausages
  • 2 tbsp Vegetable oil
  • 75g Plain flour
  • 2 Eggs
  • 150ml Milk

Directions

  1. Peel and finely slice the red onion. Cut the chicken into bite-sized pieces
  2. Heat a large, wide-based pan (preferably non-stick) with a drizzle of vegetable oil over a medium heat. Once hot, add the sliced red onion with a pinch of salt and cook for 2-3 min or until starting to soften
  3. Once starting to soften, increase the heat to medium-high, add the chicken pieces and cook for 7-8 min or until golden and cooked through
  4. Once the chicken is cooked, add the drained farfalle to the pan with the chicken and onion. Add the soft cheese, pesto and a ladle-full of pasta water. Give everything a good mix up and cook for 1-2 min or until the sauce is creamy and starting to thicken
  5. Wash the spinach, then add and stir through until wilted.
